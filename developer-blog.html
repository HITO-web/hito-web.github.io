Why I Created MapAlbum and Future Plans

Published: February 9, 2025 | Category: Developer Blog | Author: HITO

Hello, I'm the developer of MapAlbum. In this post, I'd like to share why I created this app and how I plan to develop it in the future.

Why I Created MapAlbum

My Love for Photography and Travel

I love taking photos and traveling. Visiting new places with my DSLR camera and capturing those moments through the viewfinder is one of my greatest joys.

However, the more photos I took with my DSLR, the more I faced a particular problem.

"Where did I take this photo?"
"I can't remember all the places I visited on that trip..."

DSLR photos have excellent quality, so I have thousands stored on my computer's hard drive and cloud storage. However, finding photos from a specific location is surprisingly difficult. Even when sorted by filename or date, remembering "where that beautiful scenery was" can be challenging.

While smartphone photos automatically record location information, DSLR cameras don't include GPS data unless you use a separate GPS device. Even if I take notes, linking photos to locations requires extra effort.

Frustrations with Existing Services

There were already several services that combined photos with location information. However, when I tried using them, I experienced several inconveniences.

Challenges I Found in Existing Services

  • Registration required - Creating accounts and logging in was tedious
  • Pushed toward paid plans - Free versions had limited functionality
  • Privacy concerns - Anxiety about photos and location data being stored on servers
  • Too many features - Overwhelming when I just wanted simple functionality

"I wish there was a simpler, immediately usable, and trustworthy service."

This thought became the motivation for creating MapAlbum.

What I Wanted to Achieve with MapAlbum

1. No Registration Required, Immediately Usable

I made it possible to use the app immediately upon opening a browser, without account creation or login. I valued the convenience of being able to record memories whenever inspiration strikes.

2. Basic Features Completely Free

The basic functionality of recording photos on a map is entirely free. I aimed to create a tool purely for recording memories, without the stress of being pushed toward paid plans.

3. Protecting Privacy

All photos and location information are stored in the browser's local storage. Nothing is uploaded to servers. I value the principle that your data stays in your hands.

4. Simple and User-Friendly

I designed it to work with just the simple operation of "adding photos to a map." I minimized complex settings and unused features, prioritizing intuitive use.

Development Concept:
"Create a service I truly want to use"

This is the fundamental idea behind MapAlbum's development. I first created something I could be satisfied with, then wanted to share it with others who have similar concerns.

Future Plans

MapAlbum has just begun. I plan to continue improving it continuously.

ğŸ“± Short-term Plans (Within a Few Months)

  • Increase daily upload limit - Relaxing restrictions to record more photos
  • Usability improvements - Refining the interface for better user experience
  • Bug fixes and stability - Improvements based on user feedback

ğŸš€ Medium to Long-term Plans

  • Mobile app development (iOS / Android) - More comfortable use as native apps
  • Enhanced offline functionality - Expanding features usable without internet
  • Data export features - Ability to save recorded data in other formats
  • New feature additions - Considering useful features based on user feedback

About App Development

While MapAlbum currently operates as a web app, I'm also considering development as native iOS and Android apps.

What I want to achieve through app development:

  • More comfortable user experience
  • Stronger camera integration
  • Notification features
  • Large-capacity storage using device storage

However, the core concepts of "no registration required," "basic features free," and "privacy-focused" will remain unchanged. These principles will always be maintained.

Request to Users

MapAlbum is still an evolving service. Your feedback is a tremendous help in improving the service.

  • Please let me know if you find anything difficult to use
  • Share your ideas for features you'd like to see
  • Report any bugs you encounter

Feel free to contact us through this form.

In Closing

MapAlbum was born from a simple desire: "I want to record my memories in my own way."

Taking photos, traveling, and looking back on those memories - I'll continue developing with the goal of creating a tool that enriches these experiences.
